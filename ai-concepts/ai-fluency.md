# AI Fluency

AI Fluency is the ability to work side-by-side with AI. It allows you to go further than just knowing what AI is, and instead, knowing how to apply it effectively, ethically and safely.
It involves knowing when to use AI, and when human judgement is required. It includes practical skills, knowledge and insights to adapt to the evolving AI technologies.

## AI Interaction Modes

Humans interact with AI usually on these three modes:

- **Automation:** AI completes a specific task based on human instructions (e.g., Write an email, create a photo, summarize a document)
- **Augmentation:** AI works collaboratively with a human to complete a task. It's a back-and-forth were both parties contribute to the output (e.g., brainstorming to solve a complex architectural problem for an app)
- **Agency:** AI works independently on behalf of the human. This could involve interacting with other humans or AIs. In this case, rather than defining specific actions, the human establishes the AI knowledge and behavior patterns

## The 4Ds Framework

It defines a set of competencies that ensure an effective, efficient, ethical and safe use of AI.

### Delegation

It is the ability to decide what kind of work is suitable for an AI, what is suitable to do together, and what kind of work should be done by humans.
An effective delegation requires domain expertise and understanding of AI capabilities and limitations.

### Description

It is not just about writing clever prompts. It about being able to explain tasks, ask the right questions, provide context, and guide the interaction with the AI system.

To do it you need to clearly describe what you want, and give the AI all the information it needs to deliver what you're looking for without assuming the details.

It is as important to describe how you want the task to be executed than what you want to achieve.

#### Prompting tips

- Provide context
- Offer examples
- Specify output constraints
- Break down complex tasks
- Give the AI space to think
- Define roles

### Discernment

This competency is the ability to evaluate **what** AI produces, **how** it produces it, and how it behaves. It includes evalutating the quality of the outputs and assesing how the AI arrived at its output, looking for logical errors, attention gaps, or innappropriate reasoning.

When during this step you identify issues, you need to go back to the Delegation and Description steps to refine your approach. It could be you're delegating the wrong task to the AI, or you need to provide more context or examples.

### Diligence

It means taking ownership for AI-assisted outputs. It is part of using the AI ethically and safely. It includes taking responsibility for any errors or issues that may arise.

It also means to be open and accurate about the AI interaction with relevant stakeholders.
